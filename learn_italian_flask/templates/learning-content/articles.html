Masculine/Feminine Words

In Italian, nouns and adjectives can either be masculine or feminine. This usually can be determined by the last letter of a noun. For singular items, if the noun ends with -o, it is generally masculine noun and if the noun ends in -a, it is generally feminine noun. For plural nouns, if the word is ending in -i, it is generally masculine while nouns ending in -e are feminine nouns.

Definite Articles

Definite articles are used to refer to a specific noun. Within the English language, ‘the’ is used to specify a particular noun. Where English only has one definite article, the Italian Language consists of a total of seven definite articles.

Masculine

Singular Plural
Begins with a Consonant il i
Begins with a s+consonant, ps, pn, gn, x, y or z lo gli
Begins with a Vowel l' gli

Feminine

Singular Plural
Begins with a Consonant la le
Begins with a Vowel l' le

Indefinite Articles

Indefinite articles are used to define a non specific item. In English, there are two indefinite articles ‘a’ and ‘an’, whereas the Italian Language has four indefinite articles.

Masculine

Singular
Begins with a Consonant or Vowel un
Begins with a s+consonant, ps, pn, gn, x, y or z uno

Feminine

Singular
Begins with a Consonant una
Begins with a Vowel un'

Examples

Definite Aritcles

l ragazzo / i ragazzi
The boy / the boys

Lo studente / gli studenti
The student / the students

L’amico / gli amici
The male friend / the male friends

La ragazza / le ragazze
The girl / the girls

L’amica / le amiche
The female friend / the female friends

Indefinite Articles

Un libro
A book

Uno stato
A state

Una penna
A pen

Un’ universita
A university
